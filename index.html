COCAINE COAST

The Coast Guard's 1980s War on Drugs & the SES Program

In the 1980s, South Florida and the Caribbean were the front lines of a new kind of war. As the "Cocaine Cowboys" flooded the U.S. with narcotics, the Reagan administration declared a "War on Drugs." The U.S. Coast Guard was tasked with interdicting the endless "go-fast" boats and smuggling ships. To fight speed with speed, the Coast Guard deployed a radical new asset: a trio of experimental Surface Effect Ships (SES). This is the story of that high-tech, high-stakes conflict, and the men who served in the elite unit, including two officers whose careers would overlap: Jim Howe and Wayne Justice.

KEY PERSONNEL: SES DIVISION

LT Jim Howe

Executive Officer (XO), "Red Crew"

Author of Red Crew: Fighting the War on Drugs with Reagan's Coast Guard, detailing his first-hand (1982-1985) experiences on the SES fleet.

RADM Wayne Justice

Commanding Officer (CO)

Commanded USCGC SHEARWATER (WSES-3). A highly decorated officer whose career seizures totaled over 140 tons of narcotics.

Timeline Key

General Drug War SES Program Jim Howe Wayne Justice
LATE 1970s - EARLY 1980s

The "Cocaine Cowboys" Era

Smuggling of marijuana and cocaine into South Florida explodes. Lightly regulated waters and high-speed "go-fast" boats overwhelm law enforcement, leading to a crisis.

1981 - 1982

Testing the "Silver Bullet"

The Coast Guard tests the first Surface Effect Ship, USCGC DORADO (WSES-1). The vessel—a hybrid catamaran that rides on a cushion of air—proves effective, capable of speeds over 30 knots.

SUMMER 1982

The SES Fleet is Acquired

The Coast Guard purchases three SES hulls:
• USCGC SEA HAWK (WSES-2)
• USCGC SHEARWATER (WSES-3)
• USCGC PETREL (WSES-4)

NOV 1, 1982

SES DIVISION (KEY WEST) ESTABLISHED

The Coast Guard Surface Effect Ship Division is established in Key West, FL. To maximize sea time, a multi-crewing concept is used: four 16-man crews (Red, Blue, Green, Gold) rotate among the three SES hulls. This small, elite unit is the tip of the spear in the drug war.

~1982 - 1983

LT Jim Howe, Red Crew Operations

As a junior officer, Jim Howe begins serving in the newly-formed SES Division, participating in high-speed pursuits as part of the 'Red Crew'.

~1981 - 1983

Wayne Justice, CO "Shearwater"

Wayne Justice commands the USCGC SHEARWATER (WSES-3) during its initial shakedown and operations. As CO of a core SES hull, he pioneers the high-speed interdiction tactics that Red Crew and other crews would use.

~1983 - 1985

LT Jim Howe, XO "Red Crew"

Howe serves as Executive Officer (XO) of the 'Red Crew', rotating with the flotilla (Shearwater, Petrel, etc.) during the peak of their operations. His first-hand account details these missions, where his crew personally interdicted ~18 vessels.

~1983 - 1984

Wayne Justice, CO "Cape Shoalwater"

Justice takes command of the USCGC CAPE SHOALWATER (WPB-95324). Based in West Palm Beach, his patrol boat interdicts smugglers in the Bahamas/Florida corridor, often coordinating with the SES flotilla (Howe's unit) to intercept targets.

1982 - 1985

LT Jim Howe, XO "Red Crew"

Jim Howe serves as the Executive Officer of "Red Crew," one of the four rotating crews in the SES Division. His first-hand account details high-speed pursuits, covert operations, and the intense pressure of the front-line interdiction missions.

MID 1980s

Wayne Justice, CO "Shearwater"

Wayne Justice, a 1977 Coast Guard Academy graduate, serves in the SES Division as Commanding Officer of USCGC SHEARWATER (WSES-3). He leads one of the three hulls that "Red Crew" and the other crews operate, placing him in the same small, high-stakes Key West unit as Jim Howe during this period.

1983 - 1984

Operations Wagonwheel & Hat Trick

The Reagan administration authorizes major inter-agency operations. Operation Wagonwheel (1984) and Operation Hat Trick are launched, involving the Coast Guard, Navy, and DEA to create an interdiction barrier in the Caribbean, heavily relying on the SES fleet's speed.

~1984 - 1986

Wayne Justice, XO "Dauntless"

Justice serves as XO of USCGC DAUNTLESS (WMEC-624) in Miami. From this Seventh District HQ-based cutter, he helps plan and support interdiction task forces—the very operations Howe's 'Red Crew' was executing. Both also handle significant migrant interdiction surges.

1985 - 1986

The Shift to Cocaine

Interdiction efforts, including the success of the SES fleet, make smuggling bulky marijuana bales more difficult. Smugglers shift to high-value, low-volume cocaine, which is easier to conceal. Maritime cocaine seizures rise dramatically.

1986 - 1990s

Transition to New Roles

The SES vessels were highly effective but mechanically complex and expensive to maintain. As the threat evolved, the program wound down. Justice moves to key staff roles, becoming Chief of Law Enforcement for the Seventh District, shaping policy. Howe transitions to command of larger 270-ft cutters.
